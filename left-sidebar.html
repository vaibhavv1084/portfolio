Fire Detection System

Detect Fire using Machine Learning through openCV and Python

OpenCV

OpenCV is used in a Fire Detection System to process and analyze images or video feeds, enabling the detection of fire. It helps with image preprocessing , color-based segmentation , and motion detection . OpenCV also aids in feature extraction, such as detecting contours and edges of fire, and visualizing the results by highlighting fire regions with bounding boxes in real-time.

  • Github

Why to replace a smoke detector?

During an emergency, relying solely on hardware tools for fire detection may not be ideal because hardware sensors like smoke or heat detectors can sometimes fail to detect small or early-stage fires. These tools are limited to specific conditions (e.g., smoke or high temperature) and may miss fires with minimal visible smoke or heat. Additionally, hardware systems are often static and can't be easily adjusted or scaled during a crisis. In contrast, a software-based system can quickly adapt, analyze multiple factors, and provide more accurate, real-time detection and alerts, improving response times in emergencies.

Using Python

In Python, OpenCV helps detect fire by processing video frames. First, it captures the video using `cv2.VideoCapture()`. Then, it converts the frames to HSV color space with `cv2.cvtColor()` and uses `cv2.inRange()` to filter out areas with fire-like colors. Next, `cv2.findContours()` detects the shape of the fire, and `cv2.rectangle()` or `cv2.drawContours()` highlights the fire regions. This allows the system to identify and display fire in real-time, making it possible to trigger alerts when detected.

Real-Time Monitoring: The system continuously processes the video frames in real-time, making it possible to monitor and detect fire as it happens. The frames are processed in a loop, and once the fire is detected, the system can take immediate action, like alerting the user or saving footage.
